\addchap{The Five Adorations}
\chapnum{---\footnote{From The Equinox Vol. 1, No. 2.}}
\begin{verse}
I \textsc{praise} Thee, God, whose rays upstart beneath the Bright and Morning Star: \\
\textit{Nowit asali fardh salat assobhi allahu akbar.}\editorsnote{This is \textbf{not} the previous line, but in Arabic. One might roughly interpret it as, \enquote{I intend to perform my [time-of-day]\footnotemark prayer; God is Great.}}\footnotetext{\enquote{Assobhi} is dawn, \enquote{assohri} is midday, \enquote{asasri} is late afternoon, \enquote{al maghrab} is after sunset, \enquote{al asha} is between sunset and midnight. Look into the five daily prayers of Islam to better understand these adorations. --- \textsc{Editor.}}

I praise Thee, God, the fierce and swart; at noon Thou ridest forth to war ! \\
\textit{Nowit asali fardh salat assohri allahu akbar.}

I praise Thee, God, whose arrows dart their royal radiance o'er the scar : \\
\textit{Nowit asali fardh salat asasri allahu akbar.}

I praise Thee, God, whose fires depart, who drivest down the sky thy car: \\
\textit{Nowit asali fardh salat al maghrab allahu akbar.}

I praise Thee, God, whose purple heart is hidden in the abyss afar: \\
\textit{Nowit asali fardh salat al asha allahu akbar.}
\end{verse}

{\raggedleft
  \textsc{Dost Achiha Khan.}
